# Subsecute — Product Marketing Context

## Product
Recurring payment automation app for Nigeria. Two pillars:
1. USD subscription management via virtual cards (Netflix, Spotify, ChatGPT, Figma, etc.)
2. Bill pay automation (airtime, data, power, cable TV — DSTV, GOtv)

## Target Audience
- Primary: Upper-middle/upper-class Nigerians (devs, designers, creators, fintech workers, founders)
- Secondary: Diaspora Nigerians managing family bills back home (15-17M abroad)
- Age: 22-40
- Income: ₦500K+/month or earning in USD

## Business Model
- Transaction fee on every subscription renewal and bill payment (no specific % disclosed publicly)
- $3 one-time card creation fee (from card issuer)
- NOT a savings app — it's a convenience/control app

## Key Differentiators
- Card-per-subscription (isolate, freeze, cancel any single sub)
- Plan sharing (manage family/team subscriptions from one account)
- Gift links (anyone can fund your subscription via a link)
- Bill pay automation (airtime, data, power, cable set-and-forget)
- Real 1-click cancel (delete the virtual card = subscription cancelled)

## Competitors
- Grey.co — general USD virtual cards, not subscription-specific
- Chipper Cash — cross-border transfers
- OPay/PalmPay — free bill pay but no recurring automation
- Rocket Money (US) — subscription tracking from bank statements

## Brand Voice
- Confident, clean, professional but warm
- Not corporate, not startup-bro
- "Your bills run themselves. You run your life."

## Current State
- Pre-launch, waitlist mode
- Landing page: subsecute.com (React SPA, Vite, Tailwind, Netlify)
- Primary action: Join the waitlist (email capture)

## Tech Stack
- Backend: Spring Boot, PostgreSQL
- Card issuer: Sudo Africa
- Bill pay: SafeHaven MFB VAS API
- Payments: Paystack
- Auth: Keycloak
